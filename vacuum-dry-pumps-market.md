<p><p>The vacuum dry pumps market includes several types, each serving distinct applications. Dry rotary vane pumps utilize rotating vanes to create a vacuum, while dry screw pumps employ helical screws for efficient gas transport. Dry scroll pumps use two interleaved spiral scrolls to compress gases, providing a compact solution. Dry claw pumps feature two claw-shaped rotors that generate vacuum without lubrication. Other types may include diaphragm and piston pumps, catering to specific industrial demands, emphasizing reliability and environmental safety.</p></p>
