<p><p>Reinsurance carriers operate primarily in two market types: life and non-life. Life reinsurance involves policies that cover mortality risks, focusing on life insurance products such as term life and whole life. In contrast, non-life reinsurance covers various risks associated with property, casualty, and liability insurance, addressing events like accidents, natural disasters, and lawsuits. Both market types help insurers manage risk by sharing potential losses, ensuring stability and solvency in their primary insurance operations.</p></p>
