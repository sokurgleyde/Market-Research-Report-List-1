<p><p>The barbecue charcoal market comprises various types, each catering to different preferences. Lump charcoal is made from natural wood and offers high heat with minimal ash. Extruded charcoal is compressed wood that burns steadily and efficiently. Charcoal briquettes are uniform, easy to light, and often contain binders, providing consistent heat and long burn times. Sugar charcoal, derived from compressed sugarcane residue, burns cleanly and adds a subtly sweet flavor to food, appealing to environmentally conscious consumers.</p></p>
