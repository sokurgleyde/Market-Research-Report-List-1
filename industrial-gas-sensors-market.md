<p><p>The industrial gas sensors market encompasses various technologies, each serving specific applications. Electrochemical sensors detect gas concentrations through chemical reactions, offering accuracy for toxic gases. Semiconductor sensors operate on resistive changes in materials when exposed to gases, providing rapid responses. Infrared sensors utilize light absorption properties of gases, ideal for measuring hydrocarbon concentrations. Photoionization detectors (PIDs) employ ultraviolet light to ionize gases, effectively detecting volatile organic compounds (VOCs) at low concentrations, thus enhancing safety in industrial environments.</p></p>
